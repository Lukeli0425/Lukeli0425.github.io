BayesiaNF: Scalable Posterior Estimation for Bayesian Inverse Imaging

Tianao Li1,2, Eugenia Y. Cao1, Sungheon Park1, Emma Alexander1,2
1Department of Computer Science, McCormick School of Engineering, Northwestern University 2NSF-Simons AI Institute for the Sky (SkAI)
arXiv Preprint, 2025
arXiv Code

Abstract

Uncertainty quantification (UQ) is vital for medical and scientific imaging and their downstream tasks. Neural fields (NFs), also known as implicit neural representations (INRs), have emerged as a powerful tool to solve ill-posed imaging inverse problems for their efficient representation, implicit regularization, and independence of training data. However, NFs generally produce a single solution without quantifying the uncertainty in the solution. We introduce Bayesian Neural Fields (BayesiaNF), a novel framework enabling posterior sampling with neural fields for principled uncertainty quantification in scientific inverse imaging. BayesiaNF models the image posterior with conditional normalizing flows (CNFs) that are conditioned on the output of a neural fields, which embeds features corresponding to spatial locations to capture spatial structures and correlations. This allows principled posterior sampling by passing randomly sampled latent variables through the CNF. We demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of BayesiaNF on three tasks in scientific and medical imaging: astronomical image restoration, compressed sensing MRI, and photoacoustic computed tomography (PACT).
